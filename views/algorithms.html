Dr. Kenneth Batcher has contributed two sorting algorithms to the computer science field. They are known as the Odd-Even Mergesort and the Bitonic Mergesort. Click on the algorithms for more information.

References:

Below are some references to Batcher's algorithms. Scroll through the list and click on any reference to learn more about how these algorithms revolutionalized sorting and searching in the Computer Science world.

The Art of Computer Programming: Volume 3: Sorting and Searching

Donald E. Knuth

This book addresses the performance of Batcher's odd-even mergesort and explains its benefits in great detail. It was Batcher's favorite explanation of his algorithm.

GPU Gems 2

Matt Pharr

Chapter 46 of this book describes how both the odd-even and bitonic mergesort algorithms are optimal since they are not data dependent.

Staran Parallel Processor

Goodyear Aerospace Corporation

Staran, and later the miniturized Aspro and later large scale MPP are high specialized purpose built super computers that employ versions of these algorithms in their design. Effort was made to allow for tool kits and compiler front ends on Staran and MPP to use them for different algorithms (beyond air traffic control) and this was somewhat successful for example satellite image processing used by MPP.

Most Common Video Game Console Graphics

Many gaming consoles (such as XBOX Playstation) employ some sort of sorting function based on these key algorithms for rapid graphics rendering.
